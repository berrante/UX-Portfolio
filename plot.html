About

Plot is an iOS app that I developed during my User Experience Design class at General Assembly. The app allows users to prioritize and manage their day-to-day tasks while also keeping track of personal wellness habits — like working-out, meditating, or reading. The app solution was based on a thorough interview and survey-based investigation into how individuals incorporate task-management systems into their lives.

The Challenge

Busy individuals like college students, new parents, and small-business owners need a way to consistently assess, organize, and tend-to their daily responsibilities because it helps them feel more productive, confident, and more likely to achieve short and long-term goals.

Presently, many individuals incorporate a number of disparate and sometimes unreliable strategies for managing their day-to-day.

Target Audience

Our participant, Emma, is a college student and who is overwhelmed by her academic and personal obligations. She feels that her to-do lists, homework assignments, and event reminders are too disorganized to keep track of.

She would like to organize her schedule and monitor her habits in an effort to feel more productive and track progress on goals she sets for herself.

Paper Prototype & User Flow

With our feature prioritization in mind, I created a paper prototype to layout basic design and functionality. These prototypes would later test the user flow from screen to screen.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

After user testing with five people, I got a sense of how users would interact with the paper prototype. From there I created very simple wireframes to start, retested the designs, and increased fidelity for the next version.

Higher Fidelity Wireframes

As the product design evolved, I built upon the existing design to include key features that were updated based on user feedback. Ultimately users found the profile page to be the most valuable decision maker so I focused on enhancing the profile viewing experience. The final profile page design in higher fidelity.

Design Reflection

This mobile design project was an eye opening experience to the entire product design lifecycle. For next steps there is still more user testing to be done, in addition to finessing the onboard experience.
